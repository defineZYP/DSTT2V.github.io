DSTT2V: Text-to-video Generation with Dynamic Transformations of Scene Conditions

Yipeng Zhang1, Xin Wang1 2, Hong Chen1, Chenyang Qin1, Yibo Hao1, Hong Mei3, Wenwu Zhu1 2,
1 Department of Computer Science and Technology, Tsinghua University 2 Beijing National Research Center for Information Science and Technology 3 MoE Key Lab of High Confidence Software Technologies, Peking University
Paper arXiv

Abstract

With the development of diffusion models, text-to-video generation has recently reached significant attention and achieved remarkable success. However, existing text-to-video methods is struggle to control the position of the subject and to perform scene transformations based on the provided prompts. To address this issue, we propose the Dynamic Scene Transformation Text-to-Video Generation method (DSTT2V). Specifically, we empoly a spatial layout fuser to control the scenes of each frames. To better present the dynamic transformation process, we introduce a mixed frequency controlnet for autoregressive generation of long videos. To guarantee the consistency between different video chunks, we utilize cross chunk scheduling during inference. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach in generating the videos with dynamic transformations of scenes.

Framework

The framework of DSTT2V. Our proposed method contains three main stages. The first stage is tuning the UNet with simulated videos which aims to learn the knowledge that how to change the scenes. The second stage is training the Mixed Frequency ControlNet which makes our method get the ability to generate long videos. During inference stage, we propose a cross chunk scheduling mechanism which utilizes the information of the previous chunk to make the video more consistency.
